  • Vol is RICH vs realized, but for good reason. The implied/realized vol spread is elevated because the market is pricing in event risk (Iran, PCE, PMI) that hasn't resolved. Do not sell vol into this gap-up open expecting mean reversion — the VVIX level tells you the vol surface can reprice violently in either direction. The risk/reward on short vol is asymmetric to the downside.
  • Term structure in contango at elevated levels means the market is pricing sustained uncertainty, not a near-term spike. This is different from a backwardated VIX structure (acute crisis). Contango at 26 suggests the market expects vol to remain elevated for weeks, not days. Calendar spreads (long front-month, short back-month) are not attractive here — the carry is insufficient given the event density this week (PMI today, PCE Friday).

Key level: 6,700 on ES is likely a significant gamma concentration zone — a sustained break above could trigger additional dealer buying; a rejection sets up a mean-reversion short.
