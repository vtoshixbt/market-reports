RISK REGISTER — TOP 3 ACTIVE RISKS
RISK // 01
Tariff Escalation & Stagflation Feedback Loop
Mechanism
Broad tariffs (10–25% on key trading partners) are embedding cost-push inflation into supply chains. This forces the Fed to hold rates higher for longer, compressing equity multiples while simultaneously slowing consumer spending. The feedback loop: tariffs → higher import prices → sticky CPI → Fed holds → growth slows → earnings disappoint → equities re-rate lower. Today's Import Price Index and UMich inflation expectations are direct reads on this mechanism.
Trigger to Watch
UMich 1-year inflation expectations print above 4.5% (currently ~4.3%). Import prices MoM above +0.5% ex-petroleum. Any White House announcement of additional tariff rounds — particularly on semiconductors, pharmaceuticals, or European goods — would be an immediate risk-off catalyst. Watch for retaliatory measures from China, EU, or Canada.
RISK // 02
Fed Policy Error — Premature Hold or Forced Hike
Mechanism
The Fed is caught between slowing growth (which argues for cuts) and tariff-driven inflation (which argues for holds or hikes). A policy error in either direction is the tail risk: cutting too early re-ignites inflation and forces a hawkish reversal (2022 redux); holding too long tips the economy into recession. The March 2026 FOMC meeting (late March) is the next major decision point. Markets are pricing a hold — any deviation from that, or hawkish language in the statement, would reprice the entire rate curve.
Trigger to Watch
Fed speakers today (check schedule) — any deviation from the "patient, data-dependent" script. A hot UMich inflation expectations print today could force Fed officials to walk back any dovish lean. Watch 2-year Treasury yield as the real-time Fed policy barometer — a move above 4.50% would signal markets pricing a hike, which would be a significant risk-off event for equities and growth assets.
RISK // 03
Weekend Geopolitical / Trade Headline Gap Risk
Mechanism
Friday closes in the current geopolitical environment carry elevated gap risk. The Trump administration has demonstrated a pattern of major policy announcements via social media outside of market hours. A single tariff announcement, executive order, or geopolitical escalation (Ukraine-Russia, Middle East, Taiwan Strait) over the weekend can gap ES futures 1–3% at Sunday's 6 PM ET open. This is not a tail risk — it has happened multiple times in 2025–26. Holding unhedged long equity or long BTC over the weekend requires explicit acknowledgment of this risk.
Trigger to Watch
Monitor @realDonaldTrump and official White House channels through the weekend. Watch for any G7/G20 communiqués, NATO statements, or OPEC+ production announcements. ES futures open Sunday 6 PM ET is the first market reaction point — set alerts at key support levels (SPX ~5,400 and ~5,100) for gap-down scenarios. BTC Sunday open is also a key tell for overall risk appetite heading into the following week.
