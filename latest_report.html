POSITIONING
VIX at 25.09 with VVIX at 126.50 signals vol-of-vol is spiking — the options market is pricing uncertainty about uncertainty. VIX term structure in backwardation means near-term hedges are expensive but the market is not yet pricing a sustained crisis. SKEW at 145.04 confirms heavy tail-risk demand. In this environment: reduce gross exposure, do not sell puts naked, and do not chase the overnight futures bounce. If you need downside protection, use spreads — outright puts are rich. Size positions at 60–70% of normal given VIX regime. The credit market (HY OAS ~256 bps) is dangerously complacent relative to equity vol — watch for a credit catch-up trade as the primary amplifier.
STRATEGIC OUTLOOK
The asymmetry is skewed bearish until one of three things changes: (1) Hormuz reopens and oil drops back below $80, removing the stagflation premium; (2) labor data deteriorates enough that the Fed pivots dovish despite inflation — a policy error scenario that initially rallies bonds but crushes equities; or (3) earnings revisions hold and the market decides to look through the oil shock. None of these are imminent. The base case is SPX grinding toward 6,300–6,400 over the next 4–6 weeks as multiple compression continues. The bull case requires a geopolitical resolution that isn't priced. The bear case — credit stress + earnings misses — targets 5,800–5,900. Accumulate quality defensives on weakness; avoid rate-sensitive growth and energy-cost-exposed consumer names.

▸ Vol regime: Elevated and transitioning higher. VIX at 25.09 with VVIX at 126.50 is not yet a crisis print (VIX 35+ would be), but the rate of change is the concern — both spiked 12–14% in a single session. This is a vol regime shift, not a one-day spike. Expect realized vol to follow implied vol higher over the next 5–10 sessions, which means current implied vol is likely fairly priced to slightly cheap on a forward basis, not rich.
▸ Term structure in backwardation is the key structural signal. When front-month VIX exceeds back-month, the market is pricing an acute near-term risk, not a sustained elevated vol environment. This creates a specific trade-off: near-term hedges are expensive (buy them anyway if you need them), but calendar spreads — selling front-month vol against longer-dated protection — are structurally disadvantaged. Do not roll short vol positions into the front month.
▸ SKEW at 145.04 confirms the market is paying up heavily for downside tail protection. This is consistent with the macro backdrop but also means put spreads are more capital-efficient than outright puts right now. If you're buying protection, use 1x2 put spreads or put verticals — the skew premium makes outright puts expensive relative to the spread structure.
▸ Gamma exposure and dealer hedging: With VIX spiking and the market selling off, dealers who sold puts are short gamma and forced to sell futures into weakness — this is a mechanical amplifier. The overnight ES bounce (+0.36%) may reflect dealer rebalancing at the open rather than genuine buying interest. Watch the first 30 minutes of cash trading carefully; if the bounce fades quickly, the gamma dynamic is still negative.
▸ Positioning for today's catalysts: Jobless Claims at 08:30 ET is the binary event. A weak print (>230K) in a stagflationary environment is unambiguously bad — sell the bounce. A strong print (<200K) gives the Fed cover to stay hawkish and may not help equities much. The asymmetry favors staying hedged through the number. Consider SPX put spreads with strikes at 6,500/6,300 for the next 2–3 weeks as a core hedge structure.
