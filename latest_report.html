RISK // 01
NFP / Labor Market Deterioration Triggering Fed Repricing
Mechanism
A significant NFP miss (sub-100K) combined with rising unemployment would force markets to price in emergency Fed cuts — initially equity-positive but rapidly transitioning to a growth scare. The transmission is: weak jobs → recession fears → credit spread widening → multiple compression → equity selloff. The speed of this transmission has accelerated in recent cycles as algo-driven repricing dominates the first 30 minutes post-release.
Trigger to Watch
NFP print below 100K on April 4 AND unemployment rate rising above the prior month's level by 0.3% or more. Secondary trigger: 10Y yield dropping below the prior month's low within 2 hours of the release (flight-to-safety confirmation).
RISK // 02
Carry Unwind via USD/JPY Breakdown
Mechanism
The yen carry trade remains one of the largest structural positions in global markets. A rapid USD/JPY decline (yen strengthening) forces leveraged carry unwind across risk assets simultaneously — equities, EM, crypto, and high-yield bonds all sell off in tandem. The BOJ's policy path is the primary driver; any surprise hawkish signal or intervention triggers the unwind. This is the "everything sells off at once" scenario that is hardest to hedge in real time.
Trigger to Watch
USD/JPY breaking below the prior month's low by more than 2 full figures (e.g., if prior low was 148.00, a break below 146.00) on elevated volume. Confirm with VIX spiking above 20 simultaneously — that combination signals the unwind is systemic, not orderly.
RISK // 03
ISM Manufacturing Contraction Deepening — Q2 Growth Scare
Mechanism
Today's ISM Manufacturing print is the first macro data point of Q2. A reading below 47 (deep contraction) combined with a deteriorating new orders sub-component signals that the manufacturing recession is deepening, not bottoming. This transmits to equities via: industrial/cyclical sector selloff → earnings estimate cuts → multiple compression in rate-sensitive sectors. It also re-opens the "hard landing" narrative that the market has been discounting.
Trigger to Watch
ISM Manufacturing below 47.0 today (April 1) with the New Orders sub-index below 45.0. If both conditions are met, watch XLI (Industrials ETF) for a gap down of more than 1.5% at the open as the sector-specific confirmation signal.
