The picture changes on two catalysts: (1) Hormuz de-escalation — any credible ceasefire or diplomatic channel opening would collapse the Brent-WTI spread, relieve inflation pressure, and allow the Fed to pivot dovish; that's the asymmetric long setup. Today's Eurozone CPI flash (expected 2.0%) and tomorrow's ECB/BoE decisions are secondary but will confirm whether the oil shock is already feeding into European inflation prints. Gold's -2.97% selloff despite active war is the most non-consensus signal in the market — dollar strength and real rate pressure are overriding safe-haven demand; this divergence will resolve, and when it does, the direction of resolution tells you everything about the next macro regime.

When VVIX outpaces VIX by this magnitude, it typically precedes a further VIX expansion — the market is pricing in the risk that vol itself becomes more volatile. Vol is likely rich vs. realized given the geopolitical uncertainty premium embedded in near-term options.
