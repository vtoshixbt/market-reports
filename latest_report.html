Iran Ceasefire Breakdown → Oil Shock & Stagflation Acceleration

RISK 02
Sticky Inflation + Fed Hold → Growth Multiple Compression
Transmission Mechanism
With core CPI at 2.5% and the Fed at 3.5–3.75%, real rates are positive but not restrictive enough to break inflation. If the next CPI print (April) comes in at 2.6%+ core, the market will price out the remaining 31% probability of a May cut and push the first cut expectation to Q4 2026 or later. This reprices the risk-free rate higher, directly compressing P/E multiples on growth stocks. NDX is most exposed — it is already -0.84% and trading at elevated multiples relative to a 4.37% 10Y.
Specific Trigger to Watch
April CPI (release ~April 10) core reading above 2.6% YoY. Intraday: UMich 5-year inflation expectations today above 3.5% would be an immediate hawkish signal.
RISK 03
Quarter-End OPEX Liquidity Crunch → Negative Gamma Cascade
Transmission Mechanism
$4.7T in options expire March 31. In a negative gamma environment (below ~SPX 6,480), market makers must sell futures as prices fall to maintain delta neutrality — this mechanically amplifies downside moves. Fund rebalancing (selling equities to buy bonds after equity losses) adds to the selling pressure. The combination of dealer hedging + fund rebalancing + thin quarter-end liquidity creates a non-linear risk: a 1% move can become a 2–3% move intraday with no fundamental catalyst. This is a structural, not fundamental, risk.
Specific Trigger to Watch
SPX cash index closing below 6,480 on any day through March 31. ES futures already at 6,542 pre-market — a further -0.95% move triggers the cascade zone. Monitor 3:30–4:00 PM ET daily for rebalancing flows.
