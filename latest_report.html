Options Market Observations
  • Vol regime: Transitioning from compressed to elevated. VIX at 25.09 is not yet in "crisis" territory (that's 35+), but the +12.16% single-session spike combined with VVIX at 126.50 signals the transition is accelerating. This is the dangerous middle zone — vol is expensive enough to hurt option buyers but not yet at the capitulation levels where selling premium becomes attractive. Do not fade this vol spike yet.
  • VVIX at 126.50 is the critical signal today. Vol-of-vol spiking +14.43% means the options market itself is becoming unstable — dealers are struggling to hedge their books, and bid-ask spreads on SPX options are widening. This is a short-gamma warning: if you are short options (covered calls, cash-secured puts), your delta exposure is moving faster than normal. Gamma risk is elevated across all strikes near current SPX levels.
  • VIX term structure in backwardation = near-term fear, not long-term structural concern. The market is paying up for 30-day protection but not pricing a sustained multi-month vol regime. This creates an opportunity: if you believe the stagflation narrative is structural (base case), buying longer-dated vol (3–6 month) while it remains relatively cheap vs. front-month is the asymmetric trade. Roll short-dated hedges into longer tenors.
  • SKEW at 145.04 (unchanged) confirms the tail risk premium is already baked in. The left tail (downside puts) is expensive relative to ATM vol. This means buying OTM puts for protection is costly — consider put spreads (buy the 5% OTM put, sell the 10% OTM put) to reduce premium outlay while maintaining meaningful downside coverage. The spread between strikes is wide enough to make this structure efficient.
  • Key gamma flip level to watch: SPX 6,500. Based on the current vol surface and the magnitude of the selloff, dealer gamma exposure likely flips from positive to negative gamma below the 6,500 area — meaning dealers would need to sell futures into weakness to hedge, amplifying any move lower. A break of 6,500 intraday could trigger a cascade. Conversely, a hold above 6,600 (current level) with vol compressing would signal stabilization.
