Options Market Observations
  • Vol regime: Transitioning from compressed to elevated. VIX at 25.09 is above the 20 threshold that separates "normal" from "stress" regimes, but well below the 35–40 levels that signal capitulation. We are in the danger zone — elevated enough to make premium selling painful, not elevated enough to signal the flush is complete. Do not call a bottom on VIX alone.
  • VVIX at 126.50 (+14.43%) is the critical read. VVIX measures the vol of VIX itself — a spike here means the market is aggressively buying VIX calls (tail hedges on top of tail hedges). This is a panic-hedging signal. Historically, VVIX spikes above 120 precede short-term vol mean reversion, but in a genuine macro shock (which this is), VVIX can stay elevated for weeks. Do not fade this mechanically.
  • SKEW at 145.04 (unchanged on day) is structurally elevated — this is not a new development but a persistent condition. The market has been pricing deep OTM put protection for weeks. The fact SKEW didn't spike further today despite the VIX move suggests the tail hedges were already in place. This is actually a mild positive: the market was positioned for downside, which limits forced selling from unhedged longs.
  • Contango term structure with elevated vol across the curve means the market is not pricing a quick resolution. In a normal risk-off event, the front of the VIX curve spikes and the back stays anchored (backwardation). Contango with elevated back-end vol means the market is pricing sustained uncertainty — consistent with a geopolitical conflict with no clear timeline. This is the vol structure of a regime change, not a one-day event.
