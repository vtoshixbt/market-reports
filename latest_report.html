Strait of Hormuz Blockade Persistence

Brent at $120+ reignites CPI above 3.5%, forces the Fed to hike in June, compresses equity multiples (P/E contraction from ~22x to ~18x implies SPX 5,400–5,600), and triggers credit spread widening as energy-intensive sectors face margin destruction. RISK 02 · MONETARY POLICY
Fed Policy Error — Forced Hike Scenario
Transmission Mechanism
The Fed is currently holding at 3.50–3.75% with CPI at 2.4% and Core at 2.5%. If oil-driven inflation pushes March CPI (reported April 10) above 3.0% YoY, the Fed faces a credibility crisis: cut and validate inflation, or hike and risk recession. An unexpected hike at the April 28–29 meeting would be a 1994-style shock — equities would reprice immediately, credit spreads would blow out, and the yield curve would flatten aggressively. The 2s10s at 48bp has limited room before inversion, which historically precedes recession by 6–18 months.
Specific Trigger to Watch
April 10 CPI print above 3.0% YoY (current: 2.4%), OR Fed funds futures pricing a hike probability above 30% for April 28–29 (currently 8%). A jump from 8% to 25%+ in hike probability would be the market's early warning signal.
RISK 03 · CORPORATE EARNINGS
Q1 Earnings Recession — Margin Compression
Transmission Mechanism
Rising energy costs (WTI +6.5% today alone) directly compress margins for transportation, manufacturing, consumer staples, and discretionary sectors. Geopolitical uncertainty suppresses capital expenditure guidance. If Q1 earnings season (beginning mid-April) delivers widespread guidance cuts — particularly from consumer-facing companies like CCL, airlines, and retailers — the market will reprice forward earnings estimates downward. A 10% EPS cut on SPX consensus implies fair value ~5,900–6,100 at current multiples. This risk is amplified by the fact that the current rally is multiple-expansion driven, not earnings-driven.
Specific Trigger to Watch
CCL (March 27) guidance cut on fuel costs, OR more than 3 S&P 500 companies in the first week of Q1 earnings (mid-April) issuing negative EPS guidance revisions exceeding 10%. Watch the Refinitiv/FactSet earnings revision ratio — a drop below 0.8x (downgrades vs. upgrades) signals the earnings recession is underway.
