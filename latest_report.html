Executive Summary
Primary Focus
The post-FOMC hangover is the dominant force today. Powell's hawkish press conference — signaling only one cut in 2026 vs. prior market pricing of 2–3 — combined with Brent crude above $102 and WTI holding $93+ on Strait of Hormuz disruption, has repriced the stagflation risk premium across the curve. ES futures are bouncing +0.36% pre-market after yesterday's -1.36% SPX close, but this is a relief rally into a dense event calendar: BABA, MU, LULU, DOCU, XPEV all report this week, and ECB + BoE decisions hit tomorrow. Do not mistake the futures bounce for a trend reversal — VIX at 25.09 (+12%) and VVIX at 126.50 (+14%) say the options market is still pricing escalation risk.
Positioning
VIX at 25 is elevated but not panic-level — it's the VVIX at 126.50 that demands attention. Vol-of-vol at that level means the market is actively buying protection on its protection; tail hedges are being layered, not removed. SKEW at 145 confirms deep OTM puts are bid. In this regime, selling vol into the bounce is a trap — realized vol is catching up to implied. Size down, keep gamma long or flat, and treat any rally as an opportunity to re-hedge rather than add gross exposure. The 2s10s steepening to 49bp with the 2Y up 10bp signals the front end is pricing out cuts faster than the long end — a classic stagflation signal that historically compresses equity multiples.
Strategic Outlook
The asymmetry is skewed bearish until one of three things changes: (1) oil breaks below $88 on a credible Hormuz de-escalation, which would immediately reprice the Fed path and relieve the stagflation narrative; (2) credit spreads widen to confirm what equities are already pricing — the current HY OAS at 350–400bp tightening is a dangerous divergence that historically precedes a sharp catch-up move; or (3) earnings from MU or BABA deliver enough forward guidance to anchor the tech multiple. Until then, the base case is a range-bound grind lower with episodic vol spikes. The bull case requires geopolitical resolution that isn't visible in current data. SPX 6,400 is the next structural level to watch.
