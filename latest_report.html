  • Vol regime: Transitioning from compressed to elevated. VIX at 25.09 is not yet in the "crisis" zone (35+), but the +12.16% single-day move and VVIX at 126.50 signal the market is aggressively buying vol-on-vol — a classic sign that institutional players are uncertain about the vol regime itself. This is the most dangerous phase: vol is expensive enough to hurt buyers but not high enough to signal a capitulation bottom.
  • VVIX at 126.50 is the key tell. When VVIX spikes faster than VIX, it means the options market is pricing a potential VIX spike — i.e., the market is hedging against a vol explosion, not just a price decline. This is consistent with a geopolitical shock (Hormuz) where the tail scenario (oil to $130+) is non-trivial. Selling vol here is a widow-maker trade.
  • VIX backwardation confirms near-term fear is acute. The front of the VIX curve is elevated above the back — the market is paying up for near-dated protection. This typically resolves in one of two ways: (1) the catalyst resolves and the curve snaps back to contango (vol sellers win), or (2) the catalyst escalates and the entire curve shifts higher. Given Hormuz uncertainty, do not assume resolution is imminent.
  • SKEW at 145.04 means OTM puts are expensive relative to calls. The market is paying a significant premium for downside tail protection. This makes buying puts expensive and makes put spreads (buying ATM, selling OTM) the more capital-efficient hedge structure. Avoid naked long puts at these SKEW levels unless you're targeting a specific catalyst (e.g., a hot CPI print).
  • Gamma exposure and dealer hedging: With VIX elevated and the market in backwardation, dealers are likely short gamma on near-dated options. This creates a feedback loop where equity declines force dealers to sell futures, amplifying moves. The SPX 6,600 level is a key watch — a sustained break below likely triggers additional dealer hedging flows. The overnight ES bounce to 6,740 may reflect dealers covering short gamma from yesterday's close, not genuine buying conviction.
