FOMC HAWKISH SURPRISE — MARCH 18–19
MECHANISM
If the March FOMC statement removes rate cut language entirely or introduces a rate hike bias, front-end yields spike, the dollar surges, and equity multiples compress rapidly. The 2-year Treasury yield is the first mover — watch for a break above the recent range high as the early warning signal. RISK // 02
TARIFF ESCALATION — RETALIATORY CYCLE
MECHANISM
A new round of retaliatory tariffs from the EU, China, or Canada in response to U.S. trade policy creates a negative feedback loop: higher input costs compress corporate margins, consumer prices rise, and the Fed is forced to hold rates higher for longer. Supply chain disruption adds a real-economy drag on top of the financial conditions tightening. Sectors most exposed: semiconductors, autos, agriculture, retail.
TRIGGER TO WATCH
Official announcement of retaliatory tariffs above 15% on U.S. exports from a major trading partner. Watch EUR/USD and USD/CNH for immediate FX market reaction — a sharp CNH depreciation signals Beijing is allowing currency adjustment as a trade weapon.
RISK // 03
CREDIT MARKET DISLOCATION
MECHANISM
High-yield credit spreads widening sharply (above 400bp) signals that institutional credit markets are pricing in a meaningful increase in default probability. This typically precedes equity market dislocations by 2–6 weeks as credit is the smarter money. A leveraged loan market seizure or a high-profile corporate default in a rate-sensitive sector (commercial real estate, private credit) could be the catalyst. The HYG/LQD ratio is the cleanest real-time monitor.
TRIGGER TO WATCH
HY spreads (CDX HY) breaking above 400bp on a closing basis. HYG ETF daily outflows exceeding $500M for 3+ consecutive sessions. Any major leveraged buyout or private credit vehicle announcing a covenant breach or restructuring.
