Yesterday's hot PPI (+0.7% MoM) confirmed the pipeline is refilling. The Fed is boxed: hold while growth slows, or hike into a supply shock. Positioning

Term structure in backwardation: near-term fear is priced, but tail hedges are getting expensive. Do not sell puts naked. Size risk at 60–70% of normal given the binary nature of geopolitical outcomes. Energy longs (XLE, refiners) and short-duration positioning are the two structural trades that work in this regime. Avoid adding equity length into the overnight bounce without confirmation above 6,700 SPX cash.
Strategic Outlook
The asymmetry is skewed bearish for equities and bullish for energy and real assets. What changes the picture: (1) credible Hormuz reopening timeline — Brent collapses $15–20 and equities rip; (2) a materially weak jobs print forcing the Fed to pivot language; (3) a credit event (HY spreads widening sharply) that forces institutional deleveraging. Until one of those three materializes, the base case is grinding lower on equities with SPX 6,400–6,500 as the next structural support zone. Gold at $4,844 is not rallying on the geopolitical shock — this is the non-consensus signal of the week. Watch it: if gold breaks higher through $4,900 while rates hold, that is the stagflation confirmation trade.
